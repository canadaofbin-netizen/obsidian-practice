# Situational Strength as a Lens to Understand Extra-Normative Work (Calderwood et al., 2023)

### 1) Metadata & Summary
* **Title:** Situational Strength as a Lens to Understand the Strain Implications of Extra-Normative Work
* **Authors:** Charles Calderwood, Rustin D. Meyer, Molly E. Minnen
* **Publication:** Journal of Business and Psychology (2023) 38:637–655
* **Summary:** Introducing "extra-normative work" (e.g., after-hours, teleworking). While aggregate strong situations increase employee strain, a facet-level perspective reveals paradoxical effects: consistency and clarity reduce strain, while constraints and consequences exacerbate it.

### 2) Research Gap & Problem
Previous studies on situational strength yielded empirical inconsistencies regarding its impact on employee strain. The problem lies in treating situational strength as a monolithic construct, leading to a gap in understanding how its individual facets uniquely influence strain in non-traditional "extra-normative" work contexts.

### 3) Hypotheses / RQs
* **Hypothesis 1-3:** Aggregate extra-normative situational strength is positively associated with compliance and employee strain.
* **Hypothesis 4-5:** Consistency/clarity are negatively associated with strain, while consequences/constraints are positively associated with strain.

### 4) Methodology Details
* **Study 1 (Vignette):** N = 243. Within-subjects design manipulating strong/weak vignettes. Random coefficients regression.
* **Study 2 (After-Hours):** N = 436 full-time MTurk workers. SEM in MPlus.
* **Study 3 (COVID-19 Telework):** N = 290 Prolific workers. SEM in MPlus.

### 5) Detailed Findings
* **Study 1:** Strong situations increased perceived strain (e.g., emotional exhaustion γ = 0.33, p < 0.01).
* **Study 2 (After-Hours):** Consistency linked to lower emotional exhaustion (B = -0.37, p < 0.01). Consequences positively associated with strain (B = 0.22 to 0.46, p < 0.01).
* **Study 3 (COVID-19):** Clarity linked to lower job stress (B = -0.24, p < 0.05). Consequences positively related to strain outcomes (B = 0.16 to 0.35, p < 0.05).

### 6) Implications
* **Theoretical:** Differing situational facets yield conflicting effects on employee well-being, shifting focus depending on environmental predictability.
* **Practical:** Organizations should avoid consequence-based messaging for extra-normative work and instead use high transparency, consistency, and clarity.

### 7) Limitations
* Reliance on cross-sectional self-report surveys (common method bias).
* Cannot establish causal directionality or short-term intra-individual variability over time.
* Lack of objective measures (e.g., coding actual emails).

### 8) Key Quotes
* "...enhancing extra-normative work by emphasizing or increasing consequences may be counterproductive due to harmful implications for employee strain."
