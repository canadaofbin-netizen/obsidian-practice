# Extending Situational Strength Theory to Account for Situation-Outcome Mismatch (Dalal et al., 2020)

### 1) Metadata & Summary
*   **Title**: Extending Situational Strength Theory to Account for Situation-Outcome Mismatch
*   **Authors**: Reeshad S. Dalal, Balca Alaybek, Zitong Sheng, Samantha J. Holland, Alan J. Tomassetti
*   **Publication**: Journal of Business and Psychology (2020)
*   **Summary**: Extends situational strength theory to explain paradoxical findings where strong work situations increased personality impacts on counterproductive work behavior (CWB). If a strong situation blocks an outlet for low task performance, employees predisposed to such behavior experience negative affect, leading to behavioral displacement into a "mismatched" alternative outlet like CWB.

### 2) Research Gap & Problem
*   **Gap**: Conventional theory suggests "strong" situations weaken the effect of personality. Prior research surprisingly found strong job-related situations *enhanced* personality impacts on CWB. 
*   **Problem**: Existing theory fails to account for a "situation-outcome mismatch"—situations where cues focus on one outcome (task performance) but reactions manifest in another (CWB). 

### 3) Hypotheses / RQs
*   **Hypothesis 1-2**: CWB-focused strength weakens personality impacts on CWB; Task-performance-focused strength *strengthens* it.
*   **Hypothesis 3-4**: This strengthening effect is mediated by negative affect (dual-stage moderated mediation).

### 4) Methodology Details
*   **Sample Size**: 369 employees (USA and India) recruited via MTurk.
*   **Variables**: Predictors: Personality (BFI). Moderators: Task-focused and CWB-focused situational strength. Mediator: Negative affect (PANAS). Outcomes: CWBO and CWBI.
*   **Models**: Simple moderation (PROCESS Template 1) and dual-stage moderated mediation (PROCESS Template 21).

### 5) Detailed Findings
*   **Match**: CWB-focused situational strength weakened the negative relationship between personality traits and CWB (e.g., Conscientiousness & CWBO: $B = -0.06$ high vs $B = -0.37$ low).
*   **Mismatch**: Task-performance-focused situational strength *strengthened* the negative relationship between personality and CWB (e.g., Conscientiousness & Constraints: $B = -0.61$ high vs $B = -0.20$ low).
*   **Mediation**: Negative affect successfully mediated this interaction, confirming the displacement theory.

### 6) Implications
Imposing a strong situation for task performance alone without addressing CWB can unintentionally trigger alternative negative behaviors due to reactance and behavioral displacement. HR must prioritize selecting agreeable/conscientious employees or actively deter CWB.

### 7) Limitations
*   Reliance on self-report data (common-method variance).
*   The mediator and outcomes were both measured at Time 2, precluding strong causal inferences for the second stage.
*   Only measured clarity and constraints facets to keep survey short.

### 8) Key Quotes
*   "In other words, imposing a strong situation will backfire when there is a mismatch between the situational cues and the performance domains of interest."
