# Hydraulic Displacement of Restrictive Constraints
**Core Idea:** Rigid situational constraints do not destroy behavioral variance; they pressurize and laterally displace it.

When rigid constraints (as defined by [[Paper - Meyer 2009]]) are applied, they block natural trait expression (creating a 'Situation-Outcome Mismatch' per [[Paper - Dalal 2020]]). The blocked trait generates acute negative affect, which acts as a hydraulic press. Instead of complying, the psychological energy is displaced into highly specific Counterproductive Work Behaviors (CWB). 
In essence, the very tool used to enforce baseline performance (constraints) acts as the catalyst for institutional sabotage (CWB).
