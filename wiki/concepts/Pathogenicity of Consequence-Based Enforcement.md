# Pathogenicity of Consequence-Based Enforcement
**Core Idea:** Threat-based penalties designed to enforce compliance invert into systemic pathogens that accelerate burnout and rebellion.

[[Paper - Meyer 2009]] models severe consequences as essential stabilizing forces for baseline compliance. However, when these rigid consequences are injected into fluid or autonomous workflows, they trigger a non-linear tipping point. In rigid systems, this triggers acute frustration and reactance ([[Paper - Dalal 2020]]); in boundaryless systems (extra-normative work), it chronicizes into severe emotional exhaustion and allostatic collapse ([[Paper - Calderwood 2023]]). The threat of penalty morphs into the exact catalyst for the counterproductive behaviors and burnout it was designed to suppress.
